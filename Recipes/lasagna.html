Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is one of those recipes that become the favorites of everyone who prepares them for the first time and also the one that has the versatility of being able to performed not only with meat, but with a wide variety of foods.

Ingredients

  • 12 pieces of lasagna or garofalo lasagna
  • 2 red bell peppers
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 150 g. bacon or bacon

  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper (according to the house)
  • 250 g. natural tomatoes (approximately 1 glass)
  • 250 ml of white wine (approximately 200 ml)

Steps for lasagna meat

  1. Preheat oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  2. Boil a large pot of water. Add 2 handfuls of salt.
  3. Insert the lasagna sheets one by one into the boiling water. Do not touch them so they don't stick.
  4. After about 10 minutes, remove the lasagna sheets from the water and spread them on blotting paper.
  5. Wash all the vegetables that you will use for the garnish. In the recipe, I recommend filling with the classic Bolognese sauce, carrots, garlic, bell peppers, and onions.
  6. Assemble the lasagna by layering the lasagna sheets, sauce, ricotta cheese, and Parmesan cheese.
  7. Bake the lasagna in the oven for 30 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  8. Let the lasagna cool for a few minutes before serving.

Steps for stuffed

  1. Preheat oven to 200 degrees Celsius.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, Parmesan cheese, egg, and 1/2 teaspoon salt. Set aside.
  3. In a large saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Add the ground beef and cook until browned, about 10 minutes. Drain any excess fat.
  4. Add the tomato sauce, oregano, salt, and pepper to the saucepan and bring to a simmer. Reduce heat and simmer for 15 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
  5. To assemble the lasagna, spread a thin layer of sauce on the bottom of a 9x13 inch baking dish. Top with a layer of lasagna noodles, then a layer of ricotta cheese mixture. Repeat layers until the dish is full, ending with a layer of sauce.
  6. Cover the lasagna with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  7. Let the lasagna cool for 10 minutes before serving.
